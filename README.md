# The PaCoDu Vault

## An AI-Assisted Conceptual Framework for Cost-Escalation Authentication and Adaptive Defensive Architecture

---

## Overview

The PaCoDu Vault is an AI-assisted conceptual research white paper proposing an alternative philosophy for authentication system design.

Rather than pursuing the unattainable objective of creating systems that cannot be compromised, the framework explores whether authentication architectures can discourage successful compromise by progressively increasing the cumulative cost imposed on potential attackers.

The proposed framework integrates established concepts from authentication, cryptography, biometrics, hardware-backed security, artificial intelligence, and adaptive defensive architecture into a unified conceptual model intended to stimulate discussion and future research.

---

## Central Research Proposition

The PaCoDu Vault proposes that authentication systems should increasingly be evaluated according to the cumulative cost they impose on potential attackers—including time, financial investment, computational resources, specialised expertise, organisational coordination, and operational risk—rather than solely according to whether compromise remains theoretically possible.

## Research Status

This repository presents a conceptual architectural framework.

It does **not** introduce new cryptographic primitives or claim formal cryptographic proofs.

Its contribution lies in proposing a new systems-level design philosophy intended to guide future research and engineering.
